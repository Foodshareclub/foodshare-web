### Hero Image

**Specifications:**

| File Format | JPG or PNG |

```
│  │     [Photo: Community sharing food]          │  │

│  │  ┌────────────────────────────────────────┐  │  │

│  │  │                                        │  │  │

│  └──────────────────────────────────────────────┘  │
│                                                    │
└────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Content Checklist:**
- [ ] Warm, inviting food imagery (not stock-looking)
- [ ] Diverse people sharing food (or community gathering)
- [ ] Sacramento landmark or recognizable location
- [ ] Foodshare logo (white version for overlay)
- [ ] Headline text overlay (max 8 words)
- [ ] Avoid text in bottom 20% (GoFundMe UI overlay)

**Photo Options:**

1. **Community Fridge Shot**
   - Person placing food in community fridge
   - Sacramento location visible
   - Warm, natural lighting

2. **Neighbor Exchange**
   - Two neighbors exchanging food container
   - Genuine smiles, authentic interaction
   - Front porch or neighborhood setting

3. **Food Rescue**
   - Fresh produce being sorted
   - Volunteers or community members
   - Sacramento Food Bank partnership visual

4. **App in Action**
   - Phone showing Foodshare app
   - Food in background
   - Hands holding phone naturally

## Section 4: Video Script

### Campaign Video (2-3 Minutes)

**Format:** Horizontal (16:9) for GoFundMe, vertical version for social
**Resolution:** 1920 x 1080 px minimum (4K preferred)
**Frame Rate:** 24fps or 30fps

---

#### VIDEO SCRIPT: "Sacramento Shares"

**[OPENING: 0:00-0:15]**

*Scene: Aerial shot of Sacramento skyline at golden hour, transitioning to street-level shots of diverse neighborhoods*

**TARLAN (V.O.):**
"Sacramento. 500,000 people. 200,000 neighborhoods. One community."

*Scene: Quick cuts of community moments - farmers market, neighborhood block party, family dinner*

---

**[THE PROBLEM: 0:15-0:45]**

*Scene: Refrigerator opening to reveal food. Hand throwing food into trash.*

**TARLAN (V.O.):**
"Every year, the average American family throws away $1,500 worth of perfectly good food."

*Scene: Split screen - landfill on one side, empty plate on the other*

**TARLAN (V.O.):**
"While 40% of our food goes to waste, 210,000 Sacramento residents don't know where their next meal is coming from."

*Scene: B-roll of food bank lines, community fridge*

**TARLAN (V.O.):**
"Something doesn't add up."

---

**[THE SOLUTION: 0:45-1:15]**

*Scene: Tarlan speaking directly to camera in casual Sacramento setting (park, coffee shop, or home)*

**TARLAN (ON CAMERA):**
"Hi, I'm Tarlan. And for the past two years, I've been building something to fix this problem."

*Scene: Phone screen showing Foodshare app interface*

**TARLAN (V.O.):**
"Foodshare is a free app that connects neighbors with extra food to neighbors who can use it."

*Scene: Demonstration - person photographing food, posting to app*

**TARLAN (V.O.):**
"Made too much dinner? Snap a photo, post it, and a neighbor nearby claims it."

*Scene: Two neighbors exchanging food container on porch, genuine smiles*

**TARLAN (V.O.):**
"No food wasted. No neighbor goes hungry. Simple as that."

---

**[THE ASK: 1:15-1:45]**

*Scene: Tarlan on camera*

**TARLAN (ON CAMERA):**
"But before we can launch, we need to become an official nonprofit. That means filing paperwork, paying legal fees, and doing it all the right way."

*Scene: B-roll of documents, laptop work*

**TARLAN (V.O.):**
"We're raising $500 to make it official."

*Scene: Graphics showing what $500 covers*

**TARLAN (V.O.):**
"$500 for California LLC formation. 501(c)(3) filing. Everything we need to launch."

---

**[THE IMPACT: 1:45-2:15]**

*Scene: Montage of diverse Sacramento residents*

**SACRAMENTO RESIDENT 1:**
"I love the idea of sharing with my actual neighbors."

**SACRAMENTO RESIDENT 2:**
"If I can help someone just by posting my leftovers, why wouldn't I?"

**SACRAMENTO RESIDENT 3:**
"This is what community should be."

*Scene: Map of Sacramento with dots appearing across neighborhoods*

**TARLAN (V.O.):**
"Our goal? Launch in Sacramento first. Then expand across California. Then nationwide."

*Scene: Tarlan walking through Sacramento neighborhood*

**TARLAN (V.O.):**
"But it all starts here. With us. With $500. With you."

---

**[CALL TO ACTION: 2:15-2:30]**

*Scene: Tarlan on camera, warm and direct*

**TARLAN (ON CAMERA):**
"Can you chip in $5, $10, or $25 to help launch Sacramento's food sharing app?"

*Scene: GoFundMe URL displayed prominently*

**TARLAN (ON CAMERA):**
"Every dollar brings us closer to a Sacramento where no food goes to waste and no neighbor goes hungry."

*Scene: Foodshare logo animation*

**TARLAN (V.O.):**
"Foodshare. Reclaim Excess. Feed Success."

**[END CARD: 2:30-2:35]**

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                                         │
│           [Foodshare Logo]              │
│                                         │
│    gofundme.com/foodshare-sacramento    │
│                                         │
│         Donate Today                    │
│                                         │
└─────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

## Section 8: Brand Voice Guidelines

### Tone

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Warm and neighborly | Corporate or formal |
| Hopeful and action-oriented | Guilt-tripping or preachy |
| Sacramento-proud | Generic or placeless |
| Direct and honest | Vague or overpromising |
| Community-focused | Self-promotional |

### Key Messages

**Primary:**
"Foodshare connects Sacramento neighbors to share surplus food."

**Supporting:**
- "No food wasted. No neighbor hungry."
- "Built by Sacramento, for Sacramento."
- "The sharing economy, but for what matters most."
- "Reclaim Excess. Feed Success."
