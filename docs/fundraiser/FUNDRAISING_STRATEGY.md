## Executive Summary

This document outlines Foodshare's fundraising strategy combining **GoFundMe's marketplace visibility** (190M+ donor community) with **Nextdoor's hyperlocal reach** (Sacramento neighborhood network) plus **Nextdoor Foundation grant opportunities** (up to $15,000 in free funding).

### Why This Combination Works for Food Sharing

Food sharing is inherently hyperlocal - neighbors sharing with neighbors, community fridges serving specific blocks, same-neighborhood food pickup. This matches perfectly with:

1. **GoFundMe** - Provides marketplace discovery where donors actively seek causes to support
2. **Nextdoor** - Reaches neighbors who will actually USE the app (not just donate)
3. **Nextdoor Foundation Grants** - Free money specifically for neighborhood-serving nonprofits

### Funding Phases

| Phase | Goal | Primary Approach |
|-------|------|------------------|
| **Phase 1** | $500 | Grant applications + personal network |
| **Phase 2** | $11,000 | GoFundMe campaign + Nextdoor amplification |
| **Phase 3** | $500,000 | Foundation grants + corporate sponsors |
| **Phase 4** | $2,000,000 | Impact investors + global expansion |

## Section 1: Platform Strategy

### Why GoFundMe as Primary Marketplace

**GoFundMe's Unique Advantages:**

| Feature | Benefit for Foodshare |
|---------|----------------------|
| 190M+ donor community | Donors browse and discover campaigns organically |
| No platform fees | Only 2.9% + $0.30 payment processing |
| Immediate fund access | Don't wait for campaign completion |
| Social proof mechanics | Donor count drives more donations |
| Trust signals | Verification badges build credibility |
| Charity-focused audience | Donors specifically looking for causes |

**Marketplace Discovery vs. Push Marketing:**

Most crowdfunding requires YOU to bring all the traffic. GoFundMe's marketplace means donors find YOU:

- GoFundMe homepage features trending campaigns
- Category browsing (Environment, Community)
- Geographic search (Sacramento-area campaigns)
- Recommendation algorithms based on donor interests
- Email campaigns to past donors in relevant categories

### Why Nextdoor for Hyperlocal Amplification

**Nextdoor's Unique Value for Foodshare:**

| Feature | Alignment with Foodshare |
|---------|-------------------------|
| Neighborhood-level targeting | Food sharing happens block-by-block |
| Local trust network | Neighbors trust neighbor recommendations |
| Sacramento presence | Strong adoption in Sacramento metro |
| Community fridge audience | People already discussing local food access |
| Buy Nothing adjacent | Users familiar with sharing economy |

**Key Insight:** Nextdoor users are not just potential donors - they are potential USERS of Foodshare. Every Nextdoor share does double duty:

1. Raises awareness for fundraiser
2. Pre-builds user base for app launch

### GoFundMe + Nextdoor Integration

**Native Sharing Feature:**
GoFundMe campaigns can be shared directly to Nextdoor, appearing as organic posts in neighbors' feeds rather than ads.

**Workflow:**
1. Create GoFundMe campaign
2. Share to personal Nextdoor (appears to your immediate neighbors)
3. Ask early supporters to share to THEIR Nextdoor neighborhoods
4. Each share reaches new geographic areas organically

**Sacramento Coverage Strategy:**
Sacramento has 100+ Nextdoor neighborhoods. Goal: Get shares in 30+ neighborhoods within first week.

### Nextdoor Sharing Strategy

#### Pre-Launch: Build Presence (2-4 Weeks Before)

**Goal:** Establish yourself as a trusted community member, not just a fundraiser.

**Actions:**
1. **Join local conversations** - Comment helpfully on food-related posts
2. **Share food waste facts** - Position yourself as knowledgeable
3. **Ask for input** - "Building an app to help neighbors share food - what features would you want?"
4. **Post about community fridges** - Link to existing Sacramento fridges
5. **Introduce yourself** - "Hi neighbors, I'm building something to help our community..."

**Post Templates for Pre-Launch:**

```
POST 1: Introduction
Hi [Neighborhood] neighbors! I'm Tarlan, and I've spent the past two years
building Foodshare - a free app to help neighbors share surplus food instead
of throwing it away.

We're launching in Sacramento first (because this is home!) and I'd love
to hear what features would be most helpful to you. Do you ever have extra
food you wish you could share easily?
```

```
POST 2: Food Waste Facts
Did you know? 40% of all food in America goes to waste - while 210,000
Sacramento residents face food insecurity.

I'm working on a solution: a free app that connects neighbors with surplus
food to those who can use it. More details coming soon - excited to share
with [Neighborhood]!
```

```
POST 3: Community Fridge Awareness
Shout out to the community fridge at [local location]! These neighborhood
resources are amazing.

I'm building an app that will map all Sacramento community fridges and make
it easy to see what's stocked and what's needed. Stay tuned!
```

### Strategy: Grants First, Crowdfunding as Backup

**Priority Order:**

1. **Nextdoor Foundation Grant** - Could cover $500 entirely (and more)
2. **Close the Gap Matching** - Double early donations
3. **Personal network** - 10 people x $50 = $500
4. **GoFundMe campaign** - Public crowdfunding if needed

### Grant Applications That Could Cover Phase 1

| Grant | Amount | Timeline | Fit |
|-------|--------|----------|-----|
| Nextdoor Foundation Hero | Up to $5,000 | January 2026 | Perfect |
| Nextdoor Close the Gap | Matching | Through late 2025 | Good |
| Sacramento Region Community Foundation | $1,000-$5,000 | Rolling | Good |
| CalRecycle Food Waste Prevention | $5,000+ | Annual | Excellent |
| Sierra Health Foundation | $2,500-$25,000 | Rolling | Good |

### If Relying on Crowdfunding

**Minimum Viable Donor Strategy:**

| Donation Level | Donors Needed | Target Audience |
|----------------|---------------|-----------------|
| $100 | 5 | Close friends, family |
| $50 | 10 | Extended network |
| $25 | 20 | Acquaintances, colleagues |
| $10 | 50 | Community members |

**Realistic Mix:**
- 2 x $100 = $200 (closest supporters)
- 4 x $50 = $200 (friends and family)
- 4 x $25 = $100 (extended network)
- **TOTAL: $500 from 10 donors**

### Day-by-Day Tactics for $500 Goal

**Day 1-3: Inner Circle**
- Personal texts to 20 closest contacts
- Goal: $300 (60% of goal)
- Creates social proof for others

**Day 4-7: Extended Network**
- Email to full contact list
- LinkedIn post
- Goal: $150 (cumulative 90%)

**Day 8-14: Community Push**
- Nextdoor posts
- Sacramento Facebook groups
- Goal: Final $50

**Day 15-30: Sustain and Stretch**
- If goal met, communicate stretch goals
- If not met, personal follow-ups with non-responders

### "Sell for Good" Integration Plan

**What is Sell for Good?**
Nextdoor's Sell for Good feature lets users sell items and donate proceeds to local nonprofits via PayPal Giving Fund.

**Requirements:**
1. Must be a registered 501(c)(3)
2. Must be listed on PayPal Giving Fund
3. Neighbors can select your nonprofit when selling items

**Setup Steps (Post-501(c)(3) Approval):**

1. **Register with PayPal Giving Fund**
   - Visit: paypal.com/givingfund
   - Submit 501(c)(3) documentation
   - Complete nonprofit profile
   - Wait for approval (2-4 weeks)

2. **Promote to Nextdoor Community**
   ```
   Post: "Did you know you can sell items on Nextdoor and donate proceeds
   to Foodshare? Just select us as your charity when listing. Every sale
   helps us feed more Sacramento neighbors!"
   ```

3. **Create Awareness Campaign**
   - Post monthly reminder about Sell for Good
   - Share success stories when neighbors donate
   - Thank sellers publicly

**Projected Impact:**
- If 100 Sacramento neighbors sell items at avg $20 each = $2,000/year
- Zero effort from Foodshare team after setup
- Builds ongoing community engagement

## Section 8: Risk Mitigation

### Potential Risks and Responses

| Risk | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|------|------------|------------|
| Grant application rejected | Medium | GoFundMe as backup; multiple applications |
| Slow Nextdoor engagement | Medium | Diversify to Facebook groups; personal outreach |

**Priority Spending Order:**

| Priority | Item | Cost | Cumulative |
|----------|------|------|------------|
| 1 | 501(c)(3) Application (Form 1023-EZ) | $275 | $275 |
| 2 | California LLC Formation | $70 | $345 |
| 3 | CA Attorney General Registration | $25 | $370 |
| 4 | Statement of Information | $20 | $390 |
| 5 | Registered Agent (deferred) | $110 | $500 |

**If only $400 raised:** Defer registered agent service (can use personal address initially)
